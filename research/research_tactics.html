INTELLIDEN · HCI Research

Research Tactics

01

Observational Research

Watching real users interact with smart home devices in their own environment

Objective

Observe how people naturally interact with smart home devices at home — without guiding them — to identify friction points, habits, and workarounds that users themselves don't notice or wouldn't mention in a survey.

Sessions
Robert Ionescu — Primary Persona
Morning routine at home
Observed switching between 3 different apps to control lights, thermostat, and security camera. Visibly frustrated when the camera app was slow to load.
Laura Marin — Tertiary Persona
Trying to turn off the living room light
Walked to the physical switch because she couldn't find the right device in the app. Said "I know it's in here somewhere" while scrolling through a long list.
Daniel Popa — Secondary Persona
Checking home security remotely
Opened the app while traveling, missed an alert notification that had already cleared. Had no way to see what triggered it without scrolling through a log.
Robert Ionescu
Leaving the house
Had to manually check each room to make sure devices were off. Spent 2 minutes doing this. Said he wished there was a single "leaving home" button.
Key Findings
Users switch between multiple apps constantly — no single unified view of the home exists in current solutions.
Less tech-savvy users (Laura) default to physical switches because the app UI is too complex to navigate quickly.
Alerts that clear before the user checks them leave no trace — users lose awareness of what happened in their home.
A "whole house off" action is the most requested missing feature — users perform it manually every time they leave.

Objective

Key Gaps INTELLIDEN Addresses
No competitor combines AR control with smart home integration. Meta Quest has spatial UI but no device control. Google/Apple have device control but no spatial UI.
All competitors require phone interaction. INTELLIDEN's AR headset lets users control devices hands-free, without picking up a phone.
Weak access control. Existing platforms treat household members similarly. INTELLIDEN uses 4 roles: Admin, User, Limited, Guest — matching real household dynamics.
