Robert Ionescu
Works remotely as a backend engineer. Passionate about home automation — built part of his setup using Raspberry Pi and Home Assistant before switching to INTELLIDEN.
"If I can't automate it, I'll build the automation myself. My home should know what I need before I do."
Goals
  • Full granular control over every device with deep automation scripting
  • Real-time energy consumption per device, not just totals
  • Use the AR headset to inspect and adjust devices hands-free
  • Integrate weather, calendar and third-party APIs into scenes
  • Selective guest access without giving admin privileges
Frustrations
  • Google Home and Apple Home are too simplified — no scripting
  • No mature AR interface for smart home control exists yet
  • Latency when switching between platforms breaks the experience
  • Energy reports are too aggregated — needs per-device granularity
  • Difficult to manage multi-user access without exposing everything
Interests
Home Automation IoT Development AR / VR Energy Efficiency Open Source Raspberry Pi Late-night coding
Key Scenario Robert arrives home from the office. His phone detects the geofence and triggers the "Arriving Home" scene — lights turn on at 40%, thermostat adjusts to 22°C, workstation wakes up. He puts on his AR headset and walks through the apartment seeing live overlays: the fridge shows it's been open 4 hours, the bedroom lamp is still on. He pinches the lamp icon in AR to switch it off without touching anything physical.

Daniel Popa
Manages a team of 12 at a logistics company. Travels 2–3 days a week. Got into smart home after a break-in scare in the neighborhood — security and family safety are his main drivers.
"I just want to press one button and know my family is safe, the house is locked, and the lights are off."
Goals
  • Simple morning and bedtime routines, no complex setup required
  • Monitor kids' rooms remotely — camera, motion, temperature
  • Lock and unlock doors, view cameras directly from his phone
  • Instant alerts for unusual activity while traveling
  • Shared access with his wife with separate permission levels
Frustrations
  • Too many separate apps — lights, cameras, heating all different
  • Setting up automations requires reading lengthy documentation
  • Kids accidentally trigger devices through voice assistants
  • No good guest mode — doesn't want visitors to have full access
  • Notifications are either overwhelming noise or miss critical events
Interests
Family Safety Security Cameras Smart Locks One-tap Scenes Parental Controls Remote Monitoring
Key Scenario Daniel is in a meeting when he gets an INTELLIDEN alert: motion at the front door at 3:42 PM. He opens the app, sees it's his kids arriving from school, and unlocks the door remotely with one tap. He activates the "Kids Home" scene — TV turns on in the living room, bedroom doors stay locked until homework time. Everything handled in under a minute without stepping out of the meeting.

Laura Marin
Taught Romanian literature for 38 years. Moved in with her son Robert after a hip replacement. Not comfortable with technology but trusts his setup. Prefers simple, reliable interactions over complex features.
"I don't need to understand how it works. I just need to feel safe and know Robert can help me if something goes wrong."
Goals
  • Turn lights on and off from the wrist without getting up
  • Send Robert an alert with one button press in an emergency
  • See at a glance whether the door is locked and the temperature
  • Receive medication and appointment reminders on her watch
  • Feel independent at home without constantly asking for help
Frustrations
  • Too many options on screen — she gets lost and anxious
  • Text and icons are too small on most phone interfaces
  • Accidental taps trigger things she didn't intend
  • Voice commands feel unnatural — she finds them embarrassing
  • Error messages are confusing — she just sees something "broke"
Interests
Accessibility Large UI Elements Emergency Alerts Medication Reminders High Contrast Minimal Interactions Caregiver Connectivity
Key Scenario It's 11 PM and Laura can't sleep. She raises her wrist and taps the large "Lights" button on her INTELLIDEN watch face — the bedroom lamp dims to 30%. She feels cold and taps the temperature card. Robert pre-configured this to simply notify him, so he adjusts from his room without her navigating any menus. In the morning her watch vibrates with a gentle medication reminder. She taps "Done." No menus, no typing, no confusion.
